05
GROWTH
ARABLE LAND
The total world supply of arable land on exo-3 is estimated to be around 3.2 billion hectares. The use of the land is matched with the population growth curve and assumes that it will continue to grow, as technology improves helping food production per hectare. As industrialisation and urbanisation increase with population, the model predicts the appropriate amount of land use needed to create a sustainable feedback loop to preserve the optimal amount of biodiversity for the exponential humanisation.
NATURAL RESOURCES
Assessment of exo-3 concludes a substantial stockpile of resources that if managed and recycled effectively could provide opportunities for series planetary growth. To ensure proper management the model suggests that a conservative approach to mining be considered to address unnecessary destruction of the natural biosphere.
INDUSTRIAL OUTPUT
As land usage and population increase’s, industrialisation increases at the same rate to match demand. The model of this proposed growth based on the analysis of exo-3 predicts total industrialisation by 2051, however this needs to be managed efficiently and with purpose to ensure maximum benefit of land usage spread between, resource collection, agriculture use, room to live and preservation of the biosphere.
The model suggests that industrial growth needs to be managed effectively through policy, crushing the desire for profit over sustainable practices, to ensure the survival of humanity on exo-3. Without major changes to the ways of living experienced on earth, this new potential terrestrial will succumb to our own desire for unlimited growth.
POPULATION GROWTH
The driving factor in growth is resource demand to sustain the population at replacement rate. Should population grow exponentially, other areas such as industry, land use and resource extraction will be required to meet demand. On exo-3 it’s imperative that all these factors of growth be monitored carefully and limited to best preserve the potential new world as well as place limits on inefficient and unnecessary growth.
Although the perspectives of the world’s population may vary in space and time, every human concern falls somewhere within a distance and length of space and time.

The pollution generated by the initial colonisation of exo-3 will be inevitable, however by ensuring use of renewable technologies and limiting and recycling non-renewable material, pollution risks can be mitigated to lessen the impact on the natural surfaces of the untouched terrestrial.
The world3 model factors in the improvement of all systems over time as technology improves, therefore accounting for the reduction of pollutants being generated as systems improve and become less reliant on dirty methods of extraction, transportation and usage.
